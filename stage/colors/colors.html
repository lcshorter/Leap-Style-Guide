Gray Palette

The gray palette is intended to be used holistically throughout the app. It should be implemented in text as well as used as panel and element background colors.

Consistency in the building blocks of the Leap Platform will give the apps a unified and predictable feel. Use of these grays should be universal through all apps.

Reserved Colors

Reserved colors are intended to be used as indicators of states. These colors should signify a consistency to a user; when they see one of theses colors it should be immediately clear what it is trying to convey to them.

It is also important to remember that a Brand Color (discussed below) that is too close to one of the reserved colors is a common problem of design systems, be cautious of that.

Some apps have introduced colors that indicate app-specific states such as the Concert Review / Rejected / Approved colors. ** we will review these instances and figure out how to align on them.

Brand Colors

Each app has a key color, or multiple key colors used as branding elements:

  • Some are used for Background fills such as the Concert Deep-Blue, or the Courier Deep-Purples.
  • Some are used as accent or Call-to-Action colors such as FABS or buttons.

Avatar Colors

These colors are used in the default state for avatars that have a colored background and initials.
